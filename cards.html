Kingdom is a dice-building card game for 2-4 players using a standard 52 card deck (no jokers).

Each player is assigned one of the four suits as their faction (friendly suit). The goal is to build the strongest pool of dice by drawing cards and interpreting them based on their relationship to your faction.

At the end of the game, all players roll their accumulated dice. The player(s) with the highest total wins!

The collection of dice & final rolling represents the in-universe collection of tokens, that are then used to determine the final outcome of the game by shaking a bag used to collect all player tokens (identifiable by their suit), and dumping them onto the table. Traditionally, any that roll off the table are units that fled from battle, face down tokens represent dead units, and face up units represent those that survived the battle. The player with the total number of face up units wins the game.

Some people prefer to play with no-deserter rules, where tokens are instead dumped into a tray or onto a table with a raised edge to prevent tokens falling to the floor.

  • Each round, every player draws one card from the top of the deck and gains dice based on the card's effect.
  • The game continues for as many full rounds as possible so that every player draws the exact same number of cards.
  • Any leftover cards (when the deck can't be evenly divided) remain undrawn.
  • When no more full rounds are possible, proceed to the final roll.

  • All players simultaneously roll all their accumulated dice.
  • Add up the totals (d4 max 4, d6 max 6, d12 max 12, d20 max 20).
  • The player(s) with the highest total score win(s).
  • Ties are allowed. Multiple winners are declared if tied for highest.
